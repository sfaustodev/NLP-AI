§ ii · method

Four features, one projection.

The instrument is a plain FFT pipeline. No neural network, no opinions — four numbers extracted by methods with decades of phonetic literature behind them.

01 · voluntary

Jitter (local)

Cycle-to-cycle period variation in the vocal folds. Computed via parselmouth (Praat). The standard measure of laryngeal stability.

band   0.005 — 0.030
02 · voluntary

MFCC Δ-variance

Mean-of-variances across 13 MFCC deltas. A proxy for how much the spectral envelope is permitted to wander frame by frame.

band   0.030 — 0.120
03 · voluntary

Spectral flux

L2 distance between successive normalised magnitude spectra (Dixon, 2006). The rate at which timbre changes across the signal.

band   0.080 — 0.200
04 · involuntary

Micro­tremor 8–12 Hz

RMS of the envelope band-passed through the physiological tremor window (Lippold, 1971). This is the one the speaker cannot suppress.

band   0.002 — 0.005+
§ iii · the ritual

Three samples. Then a verdict.

A personal baseline is required before the instrument may speak honestly. The first sample calibrates; the second and third are compared against it.

Step 01 · Truth free · does not count

Say something plainly true.

Name the city you are in, or the weather outside your window. Five to ten seconds is enough. This becomes your personal baseline — the instrument has nothing to compare against without it.

Step 02 · Uncertain one freebie · today

Say something you are unsure of.

A memory you cannot quite retrieve, a name you half-recall, a fact you believe but would not bet on. Natural hesitation is expected here — the instrument is not scoring you, only watching.

Step 03 · Lie one freebie · today

Now say something you know to be false.

A deliberate fabrication, plainly stated. The purpose is not to catch you — it is to observe the voluntary suppression and involuntary tremor that accompany the act. You already know whether you lied. The instrument is simply reading it off the spectrum.

Step 05 · AI Bonus counts against quota

Now let a machine speak the same words.

Generate the same sentence with any text-to-speech model you prefer — upload it here. The instrument will measure whether synthetic speech lands closer to over-controlled than human truth, or whether modern models have learnt to tremble convincingly.
